# Terminology

This page locks the meaning of a few terms that can easily get mixed up.

The goal is simple:

- the docs should use the same words the same way
- product, finance, and go-to-market discussions should stay aligned

This matters because LendPay touches several domains at once:

- credit
- tokenomics
- treasury
- app distribution
- ecosystem marketing

When these terms drift, the strategy starts drifting too. So this page is meant to keep the language clean before confusion turns into bad decisions.

## Growth

In LendPay, `growth` means controlled expansion of the borrower base, partner network, and loan volume.

Growth does **not** mean:

- chasing raw wallet count without repayment quality
- opening large limits before default rate is stable

Working definition:

- growth begins after risk quality is credible
- the default-rate gate matters more than vanity volume

In other words, LendPay should treat growth as earned expansion, not automatic expansion.

If more users arrive but repayment quality deteriorates, that is not healthy growth. It is just bigger risk.

## Marketing

`Marketing` means the channels and campaigns used to attract, activate, and retain users.

Examples:

- `X`
- `Galxe`
- `Zill`
- partner co-marketing campaigns

Marketing is responsible for:

- awareness
- activation
- retention loops

The purpose of marketing is to create attention and movement around the product.

It should help people discover LendPay, understand why it matters, and take useful first actions. But marketing alone is not the same thing as product adoption inside real app flows.

## Distribution

`Distribution` means where LendPay is embedded and how users encounter the product in real usage flows.

Examples:

- merchant checkout surfaces
- partner app rails
- embedded credit entry points inside Initia apps

Distribution is **not**:

- social media posting
- quest platforms
- generic community activity

Simple rule:

- marketing brings users in
- distribution places LendPay where users naturally transact

This distinction matters because many teams confuse audience attention with product placement.

For LendPay, real distribution begins when:

- partner apps embed the credit surface
- merchant journeys route users into checkout credit
- the product becomes part of actual usage, not only content and campaigns

## Liquidity

`Liquidity` means treasury and market access around `LEND` and other supported assets.

Examples:

- treasury operations
- MiniEVM route readiness
- DEX pool participation
- reserve management

Liquidity should not be confused with:

- borrower demand
- partner distribution
- user growth

Liquidity is about asset movement, treasury flexibility, and market access.

It answers questions like:

- can the protocol route value safely
- can treasury support redemptions, reserve policy, and fee flows
- is there a usable market path for the assets involved

It does not answer whether borrowers are healthy or whether merchants want the product.

## Utility

`Utility` means the practical reasons users or partners would hold, spend, lock, or prefer `LEND`.

Examples:

- collateral
- staking
- tier unlocks
- fee discount
- voucher eligibility

Utility should not be confused with:

- borrowed principal
- the credit line itself
- pure speculation

The key test for utility is simple:

- if a user asks “why would I actually use or keep this token inside the product?”, the answer should be clear without relying on price appreciation alone

That is why LendPay should keep `LEND` attached to real actions and real benefits, not vague future promise.

## Credit Line

`Credit line` is the borrowing power granted by underwriting.

It is:

- a product output
- shaped by borrower quality and repayment behavior
- a commercial service surface

It is not:

- a token
- a reward
- a tradable asset

This distinction is important because the credit line is the actual product LendPay distributes.

`POINTS` are not the credit line.  
`LEND` is not the credit line.  
The credit line is the borrowing access produced by underwriting policy.

So when the team talks about increasing or protecting the credit line, the conversation should be about:

- borrower quality
- repayment proof
- risk controls
- product economics

not token price or reward optics.
